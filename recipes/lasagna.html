Lasagna

Description

A traditional lasagna bolognese can't be beat. It should have homemade egg noodles, homemade bolognese, béchamel, and parmesan cheese. No ricotta and no mozzarella.

If you google "traditional lasagna bolognese" you can find a lot of variations such as different ratios of ground chuck, ground pork, and veal, whether or not to add pancetta, what type of wine to deglaze with, ratios of aromatics to meat in the bolognese, etc. I've tried a lot of them and i find the ones that use the basic recipe listed above are always great.

  • 1 medium onion, coarsely chopped (1-inch pieces are fine)
  • 1 large or 2 slim carrots, coarsely chopped
  • 2 ribs celery, coarsely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, coarsely chopped
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 pounds ground beef or, if you prefer, 1 pound each beef and pork
  • 1 1/4 cups tomato paste (from 2 6-ounce cans)
  • 2 cups red wine, preferably hearty but really, anything you like to drink
  • Water as needed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • A few sprigs thyme, tied in a bundle
  • Pasta
  • BÉCHAMEL SAUCE
  • Parmesian cheese

Steps

  1. Day 1: Make the bolognese sauce
  2. Day 2: Make your pasta
  3. Next, cook your pasta
  4. Make your béchamel
  5. At last, you may assemble your dish
  6. Bake it
  7. Store leftovers
  8. Enjoy
